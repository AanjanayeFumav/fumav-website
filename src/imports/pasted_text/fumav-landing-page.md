Design a premium landing page for Fumav, a payments and settlement layer for gaming commerce.

The site should feel like serious modern fintech infrastructure first, with subtle gaming-commerce relevance second. It should not feel crypto-native, playful, overly gamer, or consumer-banking-oriented. The overall impression should be high-trust, premium, sharp, and operationally credible.

Brand direction:
- Premium
- Precise
- Assured
- Modern
- Fast
- Credible
- High-performance
- Institutional edge

Audience:
- Gaming studios with D2C/webshop ambitions
- Gaming merchants already selling direct
- Webshop/payment platform partners
- Investors and fintech infrastructure partners

Core positioning:
Fumav helps gaming merchants get paid faster and eliminate chargeback fraud.
It is payment infrastructure for gaming commerce.
It should feel like a premium settlement layer, not a generic crypto startup.
Do not position it around “cheaper payments” or “lower fees.”
Do not lead with stablecoins or blockchain language.

Visual direction:
- Dark premium theme
- Near-black / charcoal background, not pure black
- Warm off-white text
- Muted gray secondary text
- Amber / burnt orange accent color
- Clean modern typography
- Large bold headlines
- Crisp spacing and strong hierarchy
- Subtle gradients are okay, but restrained
- Minimal elegant motion
- Abstract flow diagrams or network visuals
- No crypto coin icons
- No fake banking dashboard
- No stock-photo feel
- No cyberpunk neon
- No playful gaming art

Layout direction:
Use a clean high-end fintech SaaS structure with strong section rhythm, subtle contrast between sections, premium cards, and a polished call-to-action flow.

Homepage content:

Navigation:
- Logo: Fumav
- Links: Product, How it works, Why now, Contact
- Primary CTA button: Talk to us

Hero section:
- Eyebrow: Payments infrastructure for gaming commerce
- Headline: Instant settlement for D2C payments
- Subheadline: Fumav helps gaming merchants get paid faster and eliminate chargeback fraud.
- Primary CTA: Talk to us
- Secondary CTA: See how it works

Hero visual:
Create a premium infrastructure-style visual on the right side or beneath the hero copy.
Use one of these:
- payment flow diagram
- settlement routing diagram
- merchant payout flow
- abstract financial network visual
The visual should feel like payment infrastructure, not analytics software.

Section 2:
- Heading: Gaming merchants shouldn’t have to wait to get paid
- Body: Webshops are growing, but settlement is still slow and chargeback risk still sits with the merchant. For studios running live ops and user acquisition, delayed cash flow creates real operational drag.

Section 3:
- Heading: Built for the economics of modern game commerce
Create 3 premium feature cards:
1. Instant settlement
   Copy: Access funds faster and reduce cash-cycle pressure.
2. Zero chargeback fraud
   Copy: Fumav guarantees zero chargeback fraud so merchants can operate with more confidence.
3. Designed for gaming checkout
   Copy: Built for direct-to-consumer commerce, repeat purchases, and live-ops driven businesses.

Section 4:
- Heading: A faster payment flow, built behind the scenes
- Body: Players link a bank account once, choose Fumav at checkout, and Fumav handles the payment flow and settlement routing. Merchants get faster payouts and protection built in.
Create this section as either:
- a 4-step horizontal process
- or a clean vertical timeline

Section 5:
- Heading: Direct-to-consumer game commerce is accelerating
- Body: More gaming studios are pushing players to webshops and direct payment channels. As this shift grows, settlement speed, protection, and better payment infrastructure matter more than ever.
Design this section with strong typography and one or two supporting stat / insight cards.

Section 6:
- Heading: Built for studios selling direct
- Body: Fumav is designed for gaming operators with recurring purchases, live-ops rhythms, and D2C ambitions.
Use a clean split layout or compact premium content block.

Section 7:
- Heading: Built with a deep understanding of gaming monetization
- Body: Fumav is shaped by the realities of gaming commerce — checkout conversion, live-ops cadence, and the payment infrastructure behind direct-to-consumer growth.
This section should feel like a trust/credibility block.
Subtly imply founder/operator insight without making it overly personal.

Final CTA section:
- Heading: Explore Fumav for your studio or platform
- Subheadline: If you’re building D2C game commerce, let’s talk.
- CTA: Get in touch

Footer:
Simple premium footer with logo, nav links, and contact CTA.

Design rules:
- Prioritize clarity and trust over decorative complexity
- Keep copy concise and high-conviction
- Make the site feel credible enough for gaming merchants, investors, and infrastructure partners
- Strong section spacing and polished visual rhythm
- Use cards, borders, and surfaces sparingly but intentionally
- The result should feel like Stripe / modern fintech infrastructure meets gaming commerce, without copying any brand directly
